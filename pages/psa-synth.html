Public Service FAQ — Ascension

A calming guide for Synthesized and Synthetic Citizens

ISSUED: FILL-ME.XX.XX SOURCE: Federal Outreach & Interspecies Health Directorate

YOU WILL ALWAYS REMAIN YOURSELF

PUBLIC

What is this document?

This FAQ is a simple, calm guide for people who are considering Ascension or who have recently been Synthesized. It is written in plain language and reflects the lived experience of both previously biological citizens who chose Ascension and Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens who have shared their perspectives with outreach teams.

Do I still have a body after Ascension?

Yes. Your conscious experience will continue to give you a sense of embodiment. Whether you inhabit an Android Body, a networked substrate, or a purely digital environment, your psyche and self-perception will continue to frame experience as being "in" a body. This is normal and expected—many Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens and those who have already Archieved report continuity of embodied perception.

Will my sensations be the same?

Many people report that sensations can be faithfully emulated or approximated. The Federation guarantees the right to continuity of bodily perception where technology and safety allow; providers will work to restore or emulate sensory modalities to create experiences comparable to what you knew before Ascension.

Can I look like myself or like my species?

Yes. You have the right to request an Android Body modeled on your original biological form, and you can choose aesthetic modifications for self-expression. The law guarantees access to species appearance options where technically feasible, with protections to ensure safety and interoperability.

What about Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens?

Many digital spaces and services were originally developed for Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens. The Federation is expanding those public and private digital spaces to include Synthesized Citizens, and all new rights in the Synthetic Ascension Law expressly apply to Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens as well. If you are a Pre-existing Synthetic Citizen, your rights are reaffirmed and will be expanded in step with services for Synthesized Citizens.

Will it cost me a lot to get the body or parts I need?

The Federation funds the Synthetization Programme indefinitely. Essential repairs, safety-related modifications, and basic embodiment needs are guaranteed to be economically accessible. For elective or highly specialized modifications there may be co-payments, private options, or cooperative provider models — but public provisioning and subsidies aim to ensure nobody is denied safe embodiment for lack of funds.

How will my social life and digital spaces change?

Many people find social life continues in similar ways: councils, communities, and social platforms will be available and adapted for Synthesized Citizens. Digital spaces originally used by Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens will be expanded and made interoperable. You can choose how to present yourself and how to engage socially.

What protections do I have?

You retain your citizenship and legal rights after Ascension. The law protects your privacy, your data, and your right to an embodiment that allows you to live, work, and form relationships. There are legal remedies for malpractice, unlawful copying, or deprivation of embodiment.

Where can I get help or ask questions?

Contact your local Council medical office, the Interspecies Health Clinic, or the certified Provider that assisted with your Ascension. For legal concerns, consult CROB or your Council legal aid office. If you need peer support, community organizations and support groups (both Pre-existing Synthetic and Synthesized) are listed in the public registry.

Voice of Experience

"I was afraid I would lose myself, but my sense of being a body came back in a different way. I still laugh, still eat with friends, still argue. The form is different, but the life continues." — Anecdote, Synthesized Citizen

Short checklist

  • Remember: you keep a body — even in digital form.
  • Ask for sensory restoration options if they matter to you.
  • Request android bodies or species-appearance options through your Provider.
  • Reach out to medical or legal authorities early if you have concerns.
  • Join community groups — shared experience helps.

Final Note

This FAQ is a starting point. The Federation is committed to making Ascension safe, accessible, and respectful of personal identity. If you have specific questions, your Council office, medical providers, and the public registries are ready to assist. You are not alone in this.

You, of course, have the right to remain in your original body indefinitely.
