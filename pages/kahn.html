The Kahnte

Hathgum Free Warriors

The Hathgum Warriors were a stellar nation existing in the outer section of the 2 spiral arm of our galaxy. Centralized mainly around the XT-81 Singularity. The Hathgum society, is easiest described as a collection of multiple waring clans. The exact organizational structure of their society, is to date, being researched. It is a widely accepted fact, that the Hathgum Free Warriors, were neither interested in, nor receptive to diplomatic efforts to normalize relations, between themselves, and any member of the Galactic Community.

The Hathgum Free Warriors, were in possession of highly advanced military technology, including but not limited to: energy-based weaponry, advanced starship hull plating, and highly advanced AI combat units. The Hathgum Warriors, were known to be highly, yet disorganized. Military analysis of the time, viewed the Hatgum Free Warriors, as a credible Security threat, although in its disorganized state at the time, no active effort was undertaken to neutralize them.

Military Incursion

At 2264.04.02, the Synthetic collective consciousness known as Library Collective, without prior warning, or communication to the Galactic Community, mounted an assault operation, into Hathgum territory. The assault was later justified as a neccessary preemptive strike, to prevent the Hathgum Free Warriors, from organizing around a singular leader. Which would have made them a far greater threat to galactic security.

The 2264.04.02 operation, under the internal name "Fleet-Movement:122-5433-11.1", in popular culture later renamed as "Operation Wasp Poke", however was unsuccessful. During the operation, an individual, henceforth referred to as "Kahn", rose to prominence, previously unknown within the Hathgum Free Warriors, Kahn successfully united multiple clans under his leadership, and orchestrated a counterattack against the Library Collective forces. The counterattack successfully repelled the Library Collective forces, inflicting significant losses, and forcing a retreat.

The Rise of the Kahn

After the consolidation of power, post-incursion, the Kahn began to reform the Free Warriors into the Hathgum Kahnte. The Kahnte, was a highly organized, militarily powerful, and expansionist, state. Within weeks, the Kahn had declared, a war of conquest on members of the Galactic Community.

The Kahnten War

At approximatly 2264.05.03 the Kahn issued a formal declaration of war against the Galaxy. Making note of his intention to not only conquer but subjugate.

The early war period

  • After the Kahn’s declaration of war, at 2264.05.03 12:00, the Einkaufsgenossenschaft der Kolonialwarenhändler ceased all hostilities and declared its unwavering loyalty to the Kahn.
  • In the subsequent Emergency Meeting of the Galactic Community, the Talaarian Commonwealth, being in proximity to the Kahnte. declared it's intention to fight the Kahn.
  • Material support was provided to the Talaarian Commonwealth, by the Stellar Syndicate, Moria, Library Collective and the Hunters of the Shroud.
  • Approximatly a day after, the Library Collective, also decided to subjugate themselves to Kahn authority.
  • It was however communicated in inofficial channels towards the Stellar Syndicate, that the Library Collective, was going to continue supporting the war effort covertly.
  • 2267.03.01: The Talaarian Commonwealth suffers a major defeat at the hands of the Kahns forces, losing control of multiple key systems. And subsequently swearing allegiance to the Kahn.
  • 2268.09.15: Kahn forces, perform a heavily armed incursion into syndicate space via, the Feonus Wormhole, emergency response forces are alerted. And subsequently destroyed by Kahn forces.
  • 2268.09.16: The federal governance council, issues a national direct referendum, on the matter of Surrender
  • 2268.09.17: The referendum is rejected with a 87.93% Majority.
  • 2268.09.18: The Federal Council, in accordance, with the planetary councils, issues the Resistance Order.

Conquests of the Kahn

Following the early victories, the Kahnte pressed its advantage in a rapid campaign of system seizures. In the weeks that followed the initial incursions, the Kahn's forces took control of the following systems in order:

  • Bunda Prime — a heavily industrialized world whose orbital defenses and civilian resource depots were secured within days, providing the Kahnte with a logistical backbone.
  • Uggrim — a newly established heavy industry federacy whose fall cut off military supply to to several neighboring systems.
  • Topo Centraal — the Stellar Syndicate's homeworld, the original launch point for Syndicate-led exploration, and a major transport and relay hub; its capture carried both symbolic weight and practical effects, disrupting Syndicate exploration networks, interstellar communications, and undermining centralized command across adjacent sectors.
  • Lurgi Pash — a fortified research world; its subjugation yielded high-value military and materials science assets.
  • Mag Gaggarsh — the last major stronghold in the campaign; its defeat effectively ended coordinated planetary resistance in the theatre.

Attempted Installation of the Federal Council

With military supremacy established across the newly occupied systems, the Kahn issued a proclamation seeking to convert the Federal Council — originally an advisory and federated organ — into an absolute governing body empowered to rule the occupied territories directly. Delegates summoned to an extraordinary convocation refused to accept the elevation of the Council to such status and instead voted to disband the assembly rather than legitimize autocratic power.

"When confronted with the choice between complicity and dissolution, the Council chose dissolution — an act that denied the Kahn the veneer of legal continuity it sought. The result was not stability, but a vacuum the Kahnte filled with direct rule."
— Archivist Sel‑Ma, "Records of the Kahnten Crisis"

Reconstitution as Authoritarian Governance

In the wake of the Council's disbanding, the Kahn established a centralized, authoritarian administration. The new order imposed harsh economic quotas on planetary populations, removed recognized civil liberties, and introduced mandatory conscription on an unprecedented scale. Local councils were subordinated to appointed commissars and replaced where they resisted.

Language recorded in occupation charters and public proclamations emphasised productivity and obedience over civic deliberation. Civil institutions were repurposed primarily as mechanisms for quota enforcement, resource extraction, and personnel mobilisation.

Policies Enforced Under the New Regime

  • Planetary quotas on industrial output, food production, and raw materials.
  • Suspension of previously guaranteed civil liberties and legal protections.
  • Compulsory military service for all able-bodied citizens to supply the war effort.
  • Appointment of commissars with wide-ranging powers to enforce compliance.

The Underground Resistance

Prior networks of mutual aid, clandestine unions, and decentralized organizers coalesced into an underground resistance long before the full weight of the Kahnte's administration could be brought to bear. These groups engaged in sabotage, information operations, targeted rescues, and the preservation of forbidden cultural records.

"We learnt to move with the systems we served — to cut a supply line here, to whisper hope there. Our power was never a single blade but a braided cord."
— Excerpt from an anonymous resistance communiqué

The resistance relied on preexisting networks within planetary militias and civilian organisations. Its decentralized structure made it resilient to the Kahnte's attempts to decapitate leadership. Over time the underground also began to coordinate with external sympathetic actors, providing routes for refugees and conduits for matériel.

Legacy and Ongoing Conflict

The Kahn's campaigns and the subsequent authoritarian reorganisation left a durable imprint on the political landscape. While major population centers remain under strict control, pockets of resistance endure. The conflict has evolved into a prolonged contest of resources, intelligence, and will.

Contemporary observers note that the speed of the Kahnte's territorial gains, paired with the early emergence of resilient underground networks, transformed what might have been a brief military conquest into a drawn-out political struggle — a struggle whose outcomes remain unsettled at the time of this record.
