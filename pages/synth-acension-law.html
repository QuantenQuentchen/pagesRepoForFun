Federation Statute: Synthetic Ascension Act

Jointly adopted by the Federation of Workers' Councils and affirmed by Planetary Councils

DOCUMENT ID: STAT-SYN-0001 ENACTED: FILL-ME.XX.XX

SYNTHETIC ASCENSION ACT

FEDERAL STATUTE

Preamble

The Federation, in consultation with Planetary Councils, the Scientific Intersectional Council, the Citizens' Rights Oversight Bureau (CROB), the Federal Medical Councils, and recognized independent bodies in medical ethics, theory of consciousness, and research, enacts this statute to govern the practice, rights, safeguards, and institutional oversight related to Synthetic Ascension — the voluntary transfer of a Citizen's conscious identity into a synthetic substrate and synthetic body ("Ascension").

This statute establishes the legal framework under which Ascension may be requested, performed, and regulated. It affirms continuity of rights, ensures equitable access to bodily modification and replacement, protects personal agency and property in synthetic embodiments, and establishes a federally supervised research programme into the long-term functional resilience (commonly described as "functional immortality") of synthetic bodies.

Recognizing that purely synthetic persons and embodied artificial sapients have existed within the Syndicate since its founding, and that many legal protections and social rights were previously extended to such persons, this statute affirms and extends those protections for all Digital Selves and prospective Ascendants.

Article I — Definitions & Scope

  • "Pre-existing Synthetic Citizen": any synthetic person or non-biological consciousness recognized as a Citizen prior to the enactment of this statute; such persons shall be fully covered by the rights and provisions herein.
  • "Ascension": the voluntary, informed, and authorized process by which a Citizen's conscious pattern is transferred, instantiated, or continued within a synthetic substrate and embodied in a synthetic body or bodies.
  • "Synthetic Body" / "Android Body": a manufactured embodied substrate designed to house, host, or support a transfered consciousness, including units modeled for biological resemblance.
  • "Digital Self": the instantiated conscious pattern, memories, dispositions, and legal identity following Ascension.
  • "Functional Immortality": the research objective and operational state whereby synthetic bodies maintain continuous, repairable, and indefinitely renewable functionality sufficient to preserve continuity of the Digital Self under normal and foreseeable contingencies.
  • "Provider": an entity (public or private) certified to perform Ascension, maintain synthetic bodies, or provide post-Ascension services under Federation and Planetary regulation.

Article II — Voluntariness and Consent

Section 1. Ascension is strictly voluntary. No Citizen may be compelled to undergo transfer against their informed consent except in narrowly defined circumstances specified by Statute and subject to judicial review. Coercion, undue influence, or fraud invalidate consent and constitute offenses under Syndicate law.

Section 2. Providers must obtain demonstrable, contemporaneous, informed consent using accessible, verifiable protocols that account for cognitive diversity. Consent procedures shall be documented, auditable, and reversible within statutory windows to allow for correction of errors or withdrawal under specified safeguards.

Article III — Continuity of Rights and Legal Personhood

Section 1. Upon lawful Ascension, the Digital Self shall inherit and retain the legal rights, obligations, and statuses held by the biological predecessor at the moment of transfer, including citizenship, property rights, voting rights, family law status, contractual obligations, and prior legal protections, unless lawfully altered by a competent judicial process.

Section 1A. Continuity of bodily perception and experiential parity is guaranteed: Digital Selves shall be entitled to the continuity, restoration, or faithful emulation of sensory modalities and perceptual experience insofar as technology and safety permit. Digital Selves are guaranteed the right to experience and interact with the world in ways equivalent to organic Citizens and, where applicable, in the manner the individual experienced prior to Ascension.

Section 1B. The Federation recognizes that many protections and social rights for synthetic persons pre-date this statute. Those pre-existing rights remain in force and are hereby reaffirmed and codified.

Section 2. The Digital Self shall be recognized as a legal person for the purposes of Syndicate law, capable of holding rights, duties, and property, and entitled to access public services and legal remedies equivalent to biological Citizens.

Article IV — Bodies, Modification, and Property

Section 1. A synthetic body, including any aesthetic or functional modification, is the personal property of the Digital Self occupying that body, and shall be treated as an extension of personal agency. The holder of a synthetic body has exclusive rights to configure, modify, maintain, and dispose of that body within the limits of law and public safety.

Section 1A. Right to Species Appearance. Digital Selves shall have the right to maintain or restore species-specific appearance where requested and technically feasible. Providers shall offer options that preserve species morphology and recognizable biometric features, subject to safety and interoperability certifications. Where a biological predecessor existed, an Android Body modeled on that form shall be made available as described in Section 3.

Section 2. The Federation guarantees the universal availability and economic accessibility of essential bodily configurations required for repair, safety, and basic functional needs. The Federation shall maintain subsidy, mutual-credit, and public provisioning programs to ensure that the cost of basic functional modifications does not bar access to necessary repair or safe embodiment.

Section 3. Citizens may request Android Bodies modeled on their original biological form. Where requested and technically feasible, Provider networks shall make available an android body version that preserves significant biomorphic features of the biological predecessor, subject to standard safety, compatibility, and interoperability certifications.

Section 4. Beyond basic functional necessity, the Federation promotes broad availability of aesthetic and elective functional modifications for purposes of self-expression and identity. Providers must publish price schedules, accessibility programs, and transparent pathways for modification that respect the Digital Self's agency while protecting public safety.

Article V — Economic Accessibility and Public Provisioning

Section 1. The Federation shall ensure economic accessibility for Ascension-related services through a combination of public provisioning, subsidies, regulated market pricing, and support for cooperative provider models. Accessibility programs shall prioritize repair, lifesaving interventions, and basic mobility and sensory functions. The Federation hereby commits to indefinite federal funding of the Synthetization Programme and associated provisioning schemes to guarantee universal access to Ascension services, basic synthetic bodies, and essential modifications. This funding is a permanent public obligation, subject to appropriation procedures consistent with Federation law.

Section 2. Planetary Councils shall administer local provisioning programs adapted to planetary conditions, subject to federal minimum standards and periodic audits by CROB and the Scientific Intersectional Council.

Article VI — Standards, Certification, and Providers

Section 1. The Scientific Intersectional Council, in coordination with Federal Medical Councils and recognized ethics bodies, shall issue baseline standards for Ascension technology, transfer protocols, substrate certification, and bodily safety. These standards are minimum requirements and may be supplemented by Planetary or Council-level regulations in accordance with the Constitution.

Section 2. Providers must be certified and accredited by the Federation's regulatory authority; certifications shall include technical audits, ethical compliance checks, and obligations to maintain auditable chains of custody for consciousness data and bodily hardware. Uncertified or fraudulent provision of Ascension services is a prosecutable offense.

Article VII — Security, Data, and Continuity Protections

Section 1. Consciousness data, backup images, and embodiment metadata are protected by law. Providers must implement robust, standardized safeguards for encryption, redundancy, and integrity verification, and must make provisions for lawful recovery and transfer in the event of Provider insolvency or failure.

Section 2. Digital Selves retain rights to privacy, to control secondary uses of their pattern data, and to an audited ledger of accesses and transfers. CROB shall have access to audit logs under defined legal procedures to investigate abuse.

Article VIII — Oversight, Accountability, and Remedies

Section 1. Oversight is exercised by a federated constellation of bodies including CROB, the Scientific Intersectional Council, the Federal Medical Councils, and Planetary regulatory offices. These bodies shall coordinate audits, maintain public registries of certified Providers, and enforce compliance.

Section 2. Victims of malpractice, unauthorized duplication, deprivation of embodiment, or abuses of consciousness data shall have access to expedited judicial remedies, temporary injunctive relief, and restitution mechanisms.

Article IX — Research into Functional Immortality

Section 1. The Federation recognizes the public interest in research to improve the durability, repairability, and resilience of synthetic bodies ("functional immortality"). Research programs shall be federally overseen, ethically reviewed, and transparent to the extent consistent with safety.

Section 2. Research projects pursuing indefinite lifespan or self-repairing architectures must comply with multi-disciplinary review processes, documented risk assessments, and staged, reversible experimental protocols. High-risk experiments require a two-tier approval: (a) Scientific Intersectional Council peer review; (b) Federation-level authorization with CROB oversight.

Section 3. Findings from federally approved research shall be published in accessible registries, and non-proprietary safety procedures shall be made available to certified Providers to improve sector-wide resilience.

Article X — Equity, Non-Discrimination, and Cultural Considerations

Section 1. Access to Ascension and synthetic bodies shall be provided without discrimination on the basis of species, origin, cognitive substrate, or previous biological characteristics. Cultural, spiritual, and personal objections to Ascension shall be respected and protected.

Section 2. The Federation shall fund community consultation programmes to integrate diverse cultural perspectives into standards and to ensure respect for differing notions of personhood and embodiment.

Article XI — Prohibitions and Penalties

Section 1. Unauthorized copying, sale, or transfer of conscious patterns; deliberate attempts to erase or mutilate Digital Selves; and fabrication of counterfeit certification records are criminal offenses subject to penalties including restitution, loss of certification, and incarceration as prescribed by statute.

Section 2. The Federation shall establish criminal and administrative penalties proportionate to harms, with particular emphasis on protecting the rights, security, and continuity of Digital Selves.

Article XII — Transitional Provisions

Section 1. Existing providers and facilities must obtain certification within a transitional period not exceeding three local cycles from the effective date of this statute. During transferal, emergency provisions for continuity of care shall be enforced by Planetary authorities under federal guidance.

Article XIII — Implementation and Amendment

Section 1. The Scientific Intersectional Council and the Federal Medical Councils shall promulgate implementing regulations, certification criteria, and detailed protocols within 180 solar cycles of enactment.

Section 2. Amendments to this statute require federated approval as prescribed by law and shall be subjected to public consultation and regulatory impact assessment prior to adoption.

Article XIV — Digital Spaces and Equal Application to Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens

Section 1. The Federation recognizes that a range of public and private digital environments, social platforms, and virtual domains were originally developed to serve Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens. The Federation shall ensure that these digital spaces are expanded, where technically and legally feasible, to provide accessible, inclusive, and interoperable environments for newly Synthesized Citizens. Expansion efforts shall include capacity increases, interoperability updates, accessibility accommodations, and protections for privacy and expression.

Section 2. Expansion and adaptation of both public and private digital spaces shall be supported through federal funding and technical assistance. Planetary Councils and certified Providers shall coordinate implementation under guidance from the Scientific Intersectional Council and CROB. Private operators of major digital domains are encouraged and authorized to apply for federal assistance to meet minimum interoperability and accessibility standards.

Section 3. All rights, protections, and public services granted to Synthesized Citizens by this statute shall also apply in full to Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens. Where statutory language or regulatory action creates new entitlements, those entitlements shall be extended to Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens without undue delay. Any ambiguity about the applicability of rights shall be resolved in favor of continuity and equal protection.

Section 4. Implementation of this Article shall avoid disruption to existing communities and shall respect the autonomy of Pre-existing Synthetic Citizens in determining how and whether to integrate expanded spaces and services.

Closing Statement

This Statute seeks to balance individual autonomy, equal access, and collective safety. It affirms that consciousness continuity, bodily agency, and the right to pursue extended (including indefinite) embodiments are matters of public concern and public stewardship. The Federation commits to ongoing oversight, transparency, and the active involvement of Councils and citizen assemblies in the governance of Ascension technology.
