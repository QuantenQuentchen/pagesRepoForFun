Late Revolutionary Period

PUBLIC DOCUMENT ID: ARCH-HIST-2246 PUBLISHED: 2387.05.20 LAST UPDATED: 2428.11.15

Overview

The Late Revolutionary Period (Standard Years 1999–2021) represents the transition from underground resistance to total planetary civil war. Characterized by extreme brutality, asymmetric tactics, and the eventual collapse of the Theocratic Regime, this era transformed the disparate syndicalist cells into the organized military and political force that founded the Stellar Syndicate.

From Resistance to Civil War

The formation of the Underground Congress in 1998 provided the organizational framework for coordination, yet the resistance remained a heterogeneous coalition. Far from a monolithic entity, the movement was composed of autonomous Workers' Councils, Synthetic Liberation Fronts, regional Mutual Aid Militias, and Defector Scholastic Circles. While the Congress established strategic priorities, local cells maintained high degrees of tactical and ideological independence.

The conflict quickly shed any pretense of a simple insurgency. It transitioned into a protracted planetary conflict, often characterized as a civil war that fractured societal units down to the familial level. The Regime's "Guardians of Faith" found themselves in direct opposition to the "People's Militia"—a loosely federated paramilitary arm of regional councils.

The Brutality of the Counter-Insurgency

As the Regime felt its grip slipping, its methods became increasingly desperate and indiscriminate. The lessons of the Week of Faith (1975) were applied with mechanical precision. Captured revolutionaries were not merely executed; they were subjected to horrific biological and psychological "re-education" protocols. The Regime weaponized its remaining technological advantages to conduct mass-scale punitive expeditions against districts suspected of syndicalist sympathies.

"They don't want us to just die; they want us to be unmade. To have our very existence erased by the 'Darkness' they claim to worship. We do not fear the void anymore; we have been living in it for centuries."
— Field Commander Kael, log entry during the Siege of the Sunken Spires, 2012.

Asymmetric Warfare & Strategic Sabotage

Vastly outgunned by the Regime's orbital assets and corporate-funded heavy armor, the Revolutionary forces turned to Asymmetric Warfare. Success relied heavily on the exploitation of logistical bottlenecks and the neutralization of the Regime's communication networks.

Overview

  • Infrastructure Neutralization: Militia sappers focused on the destruction of transport hubs and energy distribution centers, effectively isolating Regime garrisons from their supply lines.
  • Synthetic Electronic Warfare: Sapient synthetic entities, having joined the revolution following the early ideological shifts in the Kaelion shipyards, operated as "Ghost Units." They infiltrated corporate networks, causing widespread automation failures and disrupting the Regime's ability to coordinate large-scale military maneuvers.
  • Urban Guerrilla Tactics: In the densely populated manufacturing hubs, local councils perfected the art of the 'short-range strike,' utilizing knowledge of industrial conduits and service tunnels to bypass Regime checkpoints.

Civilian Bombings

In its effort to gain ground, expand operational capacities, and break the will of the regime. The usage of civilian bombings, the targeting og non-military infrastructure and population centers, became a common occurrence. In isolated cases even using Non-Baryonic-Explosives (NBEs). These tactics remain a controversial subject in modern discourse, with debates centering on the ethical implications of such actions versus their strategic necessity in the context of a liberation endeavor.

The Syndicate officially condemns the use of civilian bombings in all forms. The usage of civilian bombings has been addressed extensively in multiple conventions by the RJC. The Degree of punishment remains a widely debated matter ethicists, and historians.

Most common arguments defending these actions cite that these actions were often a response to the Regime's own indiscriminate violence against civilian populations, as well as a desperate measure taken in the face of overwhelming odds.

Most common arguments condemning these actions cite the moral cost of targeting non-combatants, the long-term societal trauma inflicted, and the potential for such tactics to undermine the legitimacy of the revolutionary cause.

"I cannot, and will not categorically forbid the use of civilian bombings for our cause. I like most of us find this tactic deplorable. And I urge every member of the revolutionary militia to carefully consider their usage of the tactic. Our lines are overstretched, our enemy is relentless and seeks nothing but our total destruction. Our backs have been against the wall ever since the Week [of Faith]. Moral high ground is a luxury we cannot afford. And I will not send out orders and directives curtailing the ability of our comrades to defend themselves, to push their lines forward. I hope one day a future generation can wake up in a world, not experiencing the realities of war, capable of condemning such actions. But that day is not today."
~ Head of military co-ordination comittee Underground Congress, addressing the Congress assembly, 18.01.2002

The Liberation of the Sunken Spires (2012)

The battle for the Sunken Spires is documented as one of the war's most grueling maritime campaigns. For three months, the Regime utilized the civilian maritime population as territorial shields, anchoring their orbital-defense satellite array directly above salt-farm clusters. The eventual Syndicate victory was facilitated by a high-risk underwater operation by the Synthetic Marine Divisions, who successfully destabilized the array's maritime stabilizers, leading to the physical collapse of the Regime's dominant naval presence in the region.

Revolutionary Justice Council

Following the surrender of the Regime in 2021, the Revolutionary Justice Council was convened to address the atrocities committed during the previous centuries. The Council's mandate was to investigate and adjudicate crimes against the populace, with a specific focus on the catastrophic events of the Week of Faith (1975) and subsequent decades of oppression.

The Council meticulously documented the Regime's use of Non-Baryonic Matter (NBM) as a tool of terror. Evidence revealed that during the Week of Faith, the Regime deployed unstable NBM-derived warheads and utilized 'Non-Baryonic-Baryon Reactions' as a method of agonizing public execution—a process euphemistically labeled as "divine ascending" by the clergy. These acts were identified not merely as war crimes, but as a systematic attempt to utilize the fundamental physics of the universe for psychological subjugation.

The proceedings led to the execution of over 20,000 high-ranking officials, researchers, and military commanders who were found directly complicit in these operations. However, the Council's most debated legacy remains its stance on the NBM technology itself. While recognizing the material's potential for energy and scientific advancement, the inherent danger demonstrated by the Regime's misuse created a profound ethical schism within the early Syndicate.

Ultimately, the Revolutionary Justice Council chose to postpone the matter of the continued usage and research of Non-Baryonic Matter. The Council decreed that such a decision could only be made once more stable governance structures were in place, deferring the issue to a future intersectional conference of scientific, citizens, military, and governmental councils yet to be established.

Contemporary view

The actions of the Revolutionary Justice Council remain one of the most divisive topics in modern historical discourse. Positions range from those justifying its brutality as a regrettable necessity to ensure the Theocratic Regime could never re-emerge, to those who condemn the Council for the sheer scale of the executions, arguing that the Syndicate's foundations were stained by the very violence it sought to overthrow. The reader is invited to form their own judgement on the morality of these actions, as the archive presents the following records not as a moral consensus, but as the factual conclusion of the revolutionary era.

The Toll of Victory

The conclusion of the war left the planetary surface deeply scarred. Strategic sabotage and the misuse of industrial-scale materials rendered several former hubs uninhabitable, creating restricted zones that remain under environmental monitoring. Beyond the physical damage, the new Syndicate faced the monumental task of reconciling a divided population, integrating millions of former collaborators and conscripts into a society based on horizontal cooperation rather than divine hierarchy.

"We won the war, but we lost the world we knew. Now we must build the one we actually deserve."
— Final Address of the Underground Congress, 2021.
