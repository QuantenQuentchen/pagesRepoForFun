Theocratic Oppression

PUBLIC DOCUMENT ID: ARCH-XXXX-XXXX PUBLISHED: 2425.XX.XX LAST UPDATED: 2428.XX.XX

History

At what exact point in history the theocratic regime as we know it today began to shape, is currently a matter of active research. However, it generally is assumed to have emerged in the 16th century, either as a result of the diplomatic unification or conquest by a dominant religious power. It is generally assumed that the rise of the Regime was facilitated, by both options, although other theories exist. Research on this matter is challenging, due to the lack of any reliable sources, or historic documentation. It was propagandized that the regime has always existed, and that it was given a divine mandate to rule. Internal policy was the destruction of any evidence to the contrary.

Emergence

Anecdotal and oral evidence suggests that the regime began to take it's current shape in the 16th century. The exact geopolitical landscape in which it emerged is to date unclear. However, there are strong indicators that alternative forms of governance existed before it. All of which shaped by the natural occurrence of Non-Baryonic Matter. Whether this has lead to theological components, in every pre-regime society is still a matter of active research. Due to the nature of Non-Baryonic Matter, which can only be described as 'mystical' in a time before modern research, it is, however, generally assumed so.

Soon after the theocratic regime had established itself globally, it began the distribution of religious texts, construction of religious sites, and created a mandatory global religious schooling system. Which is assumed to be the reason for the lack of a diverse language ecosystem. Divergant languages from The language of the Gods are assumed to have existed once. Their only lingustical remains today, however, are local dialects of The language of the Gods.

Reverence for Non-Baryonic Matter

Non-Baryonic Matter colloquially called 'Dark Matter' today, played a major role as the theological foundation for the regime. Sites with high concentrations of Non-Baryonic Matter were considered sacred and often became pilgrimage destinations. The regime established strict control over these sites, regulating access and rituals performed there. The clergy claimed to have a unique ability to interact with Non-Baryonic Matter, further solidifying their authority and divine status.

Myths about the divinity of Non-Baryonic Matter persisted well into the early revolutionary period. We know today that the regime's clergy were actually better understood as a group of researchers. Even if it did not disclose it publicly, the regime undertook immense efforts to research the properties of Non-Baryonic Matter. Many contradictory actions taken by the regime are directly explained by their desire to exploit Non-Baryonig Matter not only as a foundation for religious belief, but for industrial and military means too

Creation of the first Synthetic entity

Due to the danger of Non-Baryonic baryonic Reactions a deadly, and painful effect if 'Normal Matter' interacts with 'Dark Matter', research and manipulation of Non-Baryonic Matter was inherently dangerous to researchers and workers. With the regime predominantly using political prisoners, and dissidents as labor force for especially dangerous situations. A practice not only deplorable, but also inefficient, and wasteful of resources.

In 1851, the regime issued Secret Order: 1633 to create a safer system to research manipulate and control Non-Baryonic Matter. The order was issued to both internal ministries and corporations. Amongst the different effects of the order, none are as impactful as the creation of the first Synthetic entity. Altho originally created only for limited operations, specifically concerning Non-Baryonic Matter, Synthetic entities were soon commercialized and allocated in massive quantities to both the regime's military forces. And allied corporations. Increasingly tasked with more compelx and critical operations. Intelligence capacities have been expanded multiple times. The exact moment in which synthetic entities achieved sapiens is currently unknown.

After their creation, synthetic sufferd as much, if not arguably more oppression and hardship than the biological populace. With official regime Doctrine stating, synthetic lifeforms do not possess, agency, a soul, or even the constrained rights, biological lifeforms were granted.

"To increase safety and profitability, in the areas of research and productivity, it is hereby mandated that representatives of the ministries, and the economy shall conveen to discuss alternatives to the current system." ~ Secret Order: 1633, 1851.

Organization

The organizational realities of the regime, even if self described as being of religious nature. Is much better understood as a merger of Profit seeking private corporations and the church. Forming a governmental body that was organizationally inefficient, redundant, overlapping and unstable. There are no recorded cases of natural death amongst the leading figures of the regime. All of which either having been replaced by corporate interests, or by their own clergy.

Government

Day-to-day governance was facilitated by a complex beurocracy, fundamentally under the oversight of the High Clergy. While this might have been the case on paper, the reality was that there was no clear distinction between Governance, and economy. Many high-ranking officials held dual roles in both the governmental and corporate sectors. Enforcement often being delegated to private entities. Laws were selectively applied, often contradictory, often cannonicized versions of company Codes of Conduct.

High Clergy: The High Clergy was the nominative ruling authority. It elected the Supreme Priest, high clergy members were appointed for life. Any member of the High Clergy could appoint at most one Member of the Low Clergy as their successor. In reality however, these restrictions where seldom enforced, infighting, factionalism, coups, and political power plays were rampant. With recorded instances of one Member, appointing 2-10 other members directly into the High Clergy. The High Clergy was made up of 11 members.

Low Clergy: The low Clergy was the combination of all religious personals, from heads of unimportant religious sites, to massively important religious figures. Everyone considered a citizen could in theory join its ranks, after extensive theological education training, oaths, and background checks. An option a lot of citizens took, at times the Low Clergy. There were no Limits to the number of members.

Ministries: Ministries were governmental bodies under the direct and sole control of the Supreme Priest. The amount of ministries varied significantly over time, with the Supreme Priest having the power to create new ones, or abolish old ones at any time. Appointments as Ministers were largely based on political There was no formal required structure to Ministries, beyond having a Minister at its head.

Supreme Priest: The Supreme Priest was the ruling figure of the regime. The Priest had absolute authority, on all matters. Generally delegating it's personal power to Ministries.

Military Organizations

In the history of the regime there have been a variety of military, para-military, and pseudo military forces. With often overlapping and unclear authority.

Keepers of Faith: The keepers acted as the de-facto police force, enforcing both governmental laws, and private company codes alike. The Keepers were structured similarly to the Military, with similar ranks, training and equipment. It maintained it's own intelligence service the Eye of God. It was the main coercive power used by the regime in the early stages of the revolution.

Military: The military similarly to the Keepers of Faith was an amalgamation of various private military corporations and regime controlled forces. Its exact purpose is to date unclear, as it still received a large amount of resources, in a time at which no external threat to the regime existed. It is assumed that it either maintained relevance due to internal power struggles, or that the regime was operationally unable to disband it. Its main documented usage was in conjunction with the Keepers of Faith for situations requiring large-scale force. It was extensively used and expanded during the late revolutionary period. It maintained its own intelligence service the Office for Heresy.

Intelligence

Beyond the internal intelligence services of various military organizations. Different ministries and corporations had their own intelligence agencies. All of which operating in parallel, often adverserial. The historically most significant of which where:

Ministry of Care: The Ministry of Care was tasked with maintaing incarceration systems. It operated a large network of prisons, labor camps, and re-education facilities. Maintaining its own intelligence service that mainly operated within these facilities. Historically it has operated under different names, 2 of which are known, Office of Inmate Health, and Office of inmate security. It is not entirely clear of these were merely different names for the same organization, or if they were separate entities. It presented the main thread to organization within incarceration facilities.

Ministry of Surveillance: The Ministry of Surveillance was responsible for the collection and analysis of intelligence data. It operated a vast network of agents, and surveillance facilities. It is important to note that this did not give the Ministry of Surveillance any sort of primacy, in matters of Intelligence. It like all other intelligence services that operated within the regime, were of equal standing within it. As the only intelligence agency, that did not operate from within another ministry, or military entity. It is historically significant due to its sheer scale

Corporations

Corporations within the regime operated, closely, often in conjunction with the military, the High Clergy and the Supreme Priest. Many corporations held de-facto governmental power, with some corporations even holding seats within the High Clergy. Corporations were often given governmental contracts, and special privileges, in exchange for their loyalty to the regime. The largest and most influential corporations often had their own private military forces, intelligence services, and enforcement agencies.

All corporations within the regime were Capitalist entities. They operated as publicly traded operations, within a pseudo-market system. Their primary purpose was the accumulation of wealth and concentration of power.

Pseudo-Market: A pseudo-market is an economic system that is similar to the dynamics of a libertarian capitalist market. But with extremely operational ties to the government and each other. Which prevents the market to function as a normal market, as described by libertarian theory. In contemporary economic science it is assumed, that any market, within a capitalist economic organizational model, will inherently at some point degrade into a Pseudo-Market.

Theology

The theology of the regime is largely based on the reverence of Non-Baryonic Matter. Its theology was monotheistic and centered around the myth that Non-Baryonic Matter and its properties are a sign of a divine being. The regime's religious texts describe a pantheon of deities, with a supreme deity often referred to as The Divine Source. The theology emphasized obedience, sacrifice, and the pursuit of spiritual purity. It also incorporated elements of mysticism and ritualistic practices, many of which were centered around the interaction with Non-Baryonic Matter.

It is important to note that the regime did not believe in its own theology, using it as a tool for oppression, and control of the populace. To date there is no scientific evidence supporting any part of regime scripture, regime doctrine, or theology.
